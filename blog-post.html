LOD 300 vs LOD 400:
What Your Contractor
Actually Needs

Choosing the wrong LOD specification is one of the most expensive mistakes in BIM project planning. Here's a practitioner's guide.

David Harrington
May 28, 2026 · 8 min read

The BIM Level of Development (LOD) specification is supposed to simplify project communication. In practice, it's one of the most frequently misunderstood concepts in the AEC industry — and getting it wrong costs firms real money.

What LOD Actually Means

LOD is not about visual quality. It's about the reliability of information within each model element. An LOD 300 element is modeled and coordinated. An LOD 400 element is verified to the point where it can be used directly for fabrication without additional interpretation.

LOD 300: The Coordination Sweet Spot

For most design and bid-phase BIM, LOD 300 is appropriate. Elements are modeled with specific quantity, size, shape, location, and orientation. The model can be used for coordination, quantity takeoff, and as the basis for construction documentation.

"LOD 300 models are the foundation for contractor coordination. LOD 400 is the foundation for fabrication. Confusing them is what causes contractors to bring shop drawings back for redesign."

When LOD 400 Pays for Itself

LOD 400 becomes essential when fabrication happens before installation — structural steel, precast concrete, curtain wall systems, mechanical equipment, and specialty millwork. On a recent tower project, specifying LOD 400 for the curtain wall alone saved the contractor $180,000 in shop drawing revisions.

Decision Framework by Element Type

LOD 100–200: Early design, massing studies, preliminary coordination
LOD 300: Design development, coordination, contractor bidding, standard construction docs
LOD 400: Fabricated elements — structural steel, curtain wall, MEP equipment, precast concrete
LOD 500: As-built conditions, owner facility management handover

The most effective BIM execution plans specify different LOD levels for different element categories — optimizing production cost while delivering precision where it matters most.
